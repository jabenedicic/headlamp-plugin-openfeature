# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This project follows the
[**CNCF Community Code of Conduct**](https://github.com/cncf/foundation/blob/main/code-of-conduct.md),
which is itself adapted from the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/0/code_of_conduct/).
As a CNCF-adjacent, OpenFeature-ecosystem project we align our community
standards with the CNCF Code of Conduct.

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement & reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainer, **Jason Benedicic**
([@jabenedicic](https://github.com/jabenedicic)), by direct GitHub contact (for
example, a direct message or the contact address on the maintainer's GitHub
profile). Please do **not** use the security advisory channel for conduct
reports — that channel is reserved for software vulnerabilities (see
[SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md)).

If a report concerns the maintainer, or you are not comfortable reporting to
them directly, escalate to the **CNCF Code of Conduct Committee** at
`conduct@cncf.io`. Enforcement follows the process described in the
[CNCF Community Code of Conduct](https://github.com/cncf/foundation/blob/main/code-of-conduct.md),
which this project adopts. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated
promptly and fairly, and the reporter's privacy will be respected.
